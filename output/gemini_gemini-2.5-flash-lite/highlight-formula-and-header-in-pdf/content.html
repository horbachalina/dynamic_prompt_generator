How to Highlight Formulas and Headers in Your PDF

  1. Click Add New in the left sidebar and select Upload Document.
  2. Open the document in the editor.
  3. Click Tools in the toolbar, then select Highlight.
  4. Click and drag your cursor over the formula or header text you wish to mark.
  5. To adjust the highlight's position or size after application, switch to the Select tool (pointer arrow icon) and drag the handles.
  6. If you make a mistake, click the Undo button (curved arrow pointing left) to reverse the last action.
  7. Once you are satisfied with your annotations, click Done in the top-right corner.
  8. Select Download PDF to save the annotated document.
  9. Alternatively, use Save As from the Done dropdown to export your document as a Word file, Excel spreadsheet, PowerPoint presentation, or an image file.

Limitations of Highlighting Formulas and Headers in PDFs

While the Highlight tool is effective, it has certain limitations. The tool cannot alter the original PDF content; it functions solely as an overlay. For scanned PDFs, the precision of highlighting text-based formulas or headers is dependent on the quality of the Optical Character Recognition (OCR) process. Color options for highlights may also vary based on your subscription plan, meaning not all colors might be available. Furthermore, the text highlight tool cannot directly highlight non-textual elements like images; it is designed specifically for text-based content. These visual cues are therefore best applied to documents where text recognition is reliable.
