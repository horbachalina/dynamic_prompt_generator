How to Create Cross Outs in a Document for Team Review with pdfFiller

To create cross-outs in a document for team review using pdfFiller, upload your document via the Add New option in the left sidebar, open the main toolbar, and click the cross icon to mark false/not-applicable responses. This tool is ideal for team members to indicate incorrect or irrelevant information in compliance reports, risk assessments, or feedback documents without needing software installation.

What Is the Cross-Out Tool in pdfFiller?

The cross-out tool in pdfFiller creates a permanent X-shaped marker that visually suppresses text or items in a document without altering the original content. Unlike a blackout tool, it does not permanently remove text but clearly indicates false/not-applicable responses. This tool works with text paragraphs, words, and numbers in native PDFs and appears as a circular X icon in the main toolbar. It does not affect scanned documents, which require OCR processing to enable cross-out functionality.

Teams rely on cross-outs to avoid delays in validation, incorrect compliance reporting, or repeated review cycles. For example, an internal audit manager reviewing compliance reports must mark false/not-applicable responses across multiple documents to ensure accuracy. Without this tool, team members might miss critical inconsistencies, leading to delayed approvals or incorrect submissions. pdfFiller’s cross-out feature integrates with its sharing and commenting tools, allowing team members to collaborate in real-time and track annotations for consistency.

What Can Be Crossed Out in a Document?

  • Text paragraphs, words, and numbers in native PDFs can be crossed out directly.
  • Cross-outs do not work on scanned or image-based PDFs without OCR, as they require text recognition to process.
  • Cross-outs are layered overlays; the original document remains unchanged until the user explicitly uses Clear Content.
  • Embedded images or graphics cannot be crossed out, as the tool only targets text content.

How to Create Cross Outs in pdfFiller

  1. Upload your document by clicking Add New and selecting Upload Document or drag-and-drop it to the Dashboard.
  2. Open the main toolbar and click the circular X icon to access the cross-out tool.
  3. Select the text or area you want to cross-out by clicking and dragging your cursor over it.
  4. Use the Select tool (pointer arrow icon) to reposition or resize the cross-out if needed.
  5. Click Undo (curved arrow left) to reverse the last action if the placement is incorrect.
  6. Click Done in the top-right corner to save the annotated document.
  7. Share or download the annotated document for team review to ensure all members see the marked responses.

Managing Cross Outs After Placement

  • Select a cross-out by clicking it, then drag to reposition or drag the handles to resize it.
  • Delete a cross-out by clicking the Delete key (or right-click and select Delete).
  • Copy a cross-out by pressing Ctrl+C, then paste it with Ctrl+V to duplicate it.
  • Use the right-click context menu to bring selected cross-outs to the front or send them to the back if they overlap.
  • Clear Content permanently removes all annotations, including cross-outs, and cannot be undone. This action affects the entire document.
  • Cross-outs persist when shared via link or email, allowing team members to view all annotations placed by others.

What the Cross-Out Tool Cannot Do

  • Cross-outs do not modify the original PDF content; they are layered overlays that do not alter the underlying text.
  • Scanned or image-based PDFs cannot be crossed out without OCR, which requires additional processing steps.
  • Clear Content permanently removes all annotations, including cross-outs, and cannot be undone once executed.
  • Cross-outs do not affect the document’s structure or text flow; they only visually suppress selected text.
  • There are no color customization options; cross-outs are always black.
  • Cross-outs cannot be undone after saving the document, so users must review their placement before finalizing.
